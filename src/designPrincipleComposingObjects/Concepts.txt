Concepts of "Is a/an" and "Has a"

"Is a"

It reffers to a class that extends from another class, for example, we have two classes, one is Animal 
and the another one is Lion, Lion extends from Animal, then, we can say, "Lion IS A/AN Animal"


"Has a"

Here we are talking about when a Class have an instance of another class as part of it's own fields,
For example, we have the class called Tyre, and also we have the class called Car, but car have as one of it's fieldds
a Tyre instance
